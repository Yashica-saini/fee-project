About the Swiss Franc

Overview

The Swiss Franc (CHF) is the official currency of Switzerland and Liechtenstein. Known for its stability, the Swiss Franc is a strong currency that is often seen as a safe haven in times of global economic uncertainty. The symbol for the Swiss Franc is typically represented by the "Fr" symbol or simply CHF for its currency code.

History

The Swiss Franc was introduced in 1850, replacing the various local currencies that were previously used across the Swiss Confederation. It has since maintained its reputation as one of the most reliable and stable currencies in the world. The Swiss National Bank (SNB) is responsible for issuing and managing the supply of Swiss Francs.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: Fr
  • Currency code: CHF
  • Central bank: Swiss National Bank (SNB)
  • First issued: 1850
  • Used in: Switzerland and Liechtenstein

Global Significance

The Swiss Franc is one of the most traded currencies in the world. Its strength and stability have made it a popular choice for investors and central banks, especially during periods of economic uncertainty.
