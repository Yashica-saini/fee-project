About the Indian Rupee

Overview

The Indian Rupee (INR) is the official currency of India. It is regulated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and is represented by the symbol ₹. The Rupee plays a vital role in India's economy, facilitating trade and everyday transactions across the country.

History

The Indian Rupee has a long history, dating back to ancient India when silver coins called "rupiya" were used. Modern banknotes and coins were introduced in the 20th century, and the symbol ₹ was officially adopted in 2010 to give the currency a distinct identity.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: ₹
  • Currency code: INR
  • Central bank: Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
  • First issued: Ancient origins, modern banknotes from 1935
  • Used in: India and some neighboring countries

Global Significance

While the Indian Rupee is not as globally dominant as the US Dollar or Euro, it is significant within South Asia and is gaining influence as India grows in economic prominence. The Rupee is also widely used in trade with India's neighboring countries.
