About the British Pound

Overview

The British Pound (GBP), also known as the Pound Sterling, is the official currency of the United Kingdom. It is one of the oldest currencies still in use and is widely recognized for its role in international trade and finance. The symbol for the British Pound is £.

History

The British Pound dates back to the 8th century and has a rich history tied to the United Kingdom’s development as a major global power. The Bank of England, established in 1694, is responsible for issuing and regulating the Pound today.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: £
  • Currency code: GBP
  • Central bank: Bank of England
  • First issued: 8th century (modern banknotes from 1694)
  • Used in: United Kingdom and its territories

Global Significance

The British Pound remains one of the world's most traded currencies, playing a critical role in international finance, particularly in Europe. It is one of the world's oldest currencies still in circulation and continues to be a symbol of the UK's global influence.
