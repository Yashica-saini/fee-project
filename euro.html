About the Euro

Overview

The Euro (EUR) is the official currency of the Eurozone, which consists of 20 of the 27 European Union member states. It is one of the most important currencies in the world, second only to the US dollar in terms of global use. The symbol for the Euro is €.

History

The Euro was introduced in 1999 as an electronic currency, and Euro banknotes and coins were introduced in 2002. It replaced several national currencies and was established to promote economic stability and integration across Europe.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: €
  • Currency code: EUR
  • Central bank: European Central Bank (ECB)
  • First issued: 1999 (electronic), 2002 (physical)
  • Used in: Eurozone countries and a few other regions

Global Significance

The Euro is the second most widely held reserve currency globally and plays a critical role in international trade, particularly within Europe. It is a symbol of European integration and economic cooperation.
