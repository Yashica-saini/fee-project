About the Brazilian Real

Overview

The Brazilian Real (BRL) is the official currency of Brazil. The Real is denoted by the symbol R$, and its currency code is BRL. The Real is widely used throughout Brazil and plays a critical role in South America's largest economy, with Brazil being one of the leading producers of commodities such as coffee, sugar, and soybeans.

History

The current version of the Brazilian Real was introduced in 1994 as part of the "Plano Real," a stabilization plan aimed at controlling hyperinflation. The Central Bank of Brazil is responsible for issuing and managing the Real. Before this, Brazil had used several different currencies over the years due to economic instability.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: R$
  • Currency code: BRL
  • Central bank: Central Bank of Brazil
  • First issued: 1994 (current Real)
  • Used in: Brazil

Global Significance

The Brazilian Real is an important currency in Latin America, reflecting Brazil's role as a major player in the global commodities market. Despite facing economic challenges and periods of high inflation, the Real remains central to trade and investment in Brazil, which is one of the largest economies in the region.
