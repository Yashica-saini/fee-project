About the UAE Dirham

Overview

The United Arab Emirates Dirham (AED) is the official currency of the United Arab Emirates (UAE). The Dirham is abbreviated as "د.إ" in Arabic or "AED" internationally, with the symbol often represented as "د.إ". It is one of the most stable currencies in the Middle East, reflecting the UAE's strong economic position.

History

The UAE Dirham was introduced in 1973, replacing the Qatari and Dubai Riyal. The Central Bank of the UAE is responsible for issuing and regulating the Dirham. The currency is pegged to the US Dollar at a fixed exchange rate, which has helped maintain its stability and importance in international trade.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: د.إ
  • Currency code: AED
  • Central bank: Central Bank of the UAE
  • First issued: 1973
  • Used in: United Arab Emirates

Global Significance

The UAE Dirham plays a vital role in the Middle Eastern economy and international trade, given the UAE's position as a global business hub. With its economy heavily reliant on oil exports and global investments, the Dirham's peg to the US Dollar ensures stability and confidence in international transactions.
