About the Australian Dollar

Overview

The Australian Dollar (AUD) is the official currency of Australia, as well as several of its territories. It is often symbolized as A$ or simply $, and it plays a significant role in global commodity markets due to Australia's rich natural resources. The currency is known for its colorful polymer notes, which are durable and resistant to wear.

History

The Australian Dollar was introduced on February 14, 1966, replacing the Australian pound. The move was part of the country's shift to a decimal system of currency. The Reserve Bank of Australia is responsible for issuing and regulating the currency.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: $ (or A$)
  • Currency code: AUD
  • Central bank: Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA)
  • First issued: 1966
  • Used in: Australia and its territories

Global Significance

The Australian Dollar is one of the most traded currencies in the world, often used in transactions related to commodities like gold and oil. Australia's strong economic ties with the Asia-Pacific region contribute to the currency's prominence in international trade.
