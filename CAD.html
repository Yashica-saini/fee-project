About the Canadian Dollar

Overview

The Canadian Dollar (CAD) is the official currency of Canada. Known for its iconic coins featuring national symbols like the maple leaf, it is commonly referred to as the "loonie" due to the loon bird depicted on the one-dollar coin. The symbol for the Canadian Dollar is $ or sometimes C$ to distinguish it from other dollar currencies.

History

The Canadian Dollar was introduced in 1858, replacing the Canadian pound. Today, it is issued and regulated by the Bank of Canada. The currency is widely accepted across Canada and is frequently traded on international currency markets.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: $ (or C$)
  • Currency code: CAD
  • Central bank: Bank of Canada
  • First issued: 1858
  • Used in: Canada

Global Significance

The Canadian Dollar is one of the top 10 most traded currencies globally, supported by Canada’s robust economy and its rich natural resources. It plays a key role in international trade, especially with Canada’s neighbors and major partners, like the United States.
