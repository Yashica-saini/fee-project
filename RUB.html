About the Russian Ruble

Overview

The Russian Ruble (RUB) is the official currency of the Russian Federation. The Ruble is symbolized as ₽ and has the currency code RUB. As one of the oldest currencies in the world, it plays a vital role in the Russian economy, which is one of the largest in the world, particularly in energy exports like oil and gas.

History

The Ruble has a long history, dating back to the 13th century. The modern version of the Russian Ruble was introduced in 1991, following the dissolution of the Soviet Union. The Central Bank of Russia is responsible for issuing and regulating the Ruble. It has gone through several redenominations due to inflation over the years.

Key Facts

  • Symbol: ₽
  • Currency code: RUB
  • Central bank: Central Bank of Russia
  • First issued: 1991 (modern Ruble)
  • Used in: Russia, and partially in countries that border Russia

Global Significance

The Russian Ruble is closely tied to the country's energy exports, particularly oil and natural gas. It is a major currency in global commodities markets, especially in Europe and Asia. While it has faced volatility, especially during times of political and economic sanctions, the Ruble remains a key currency in global trade and finance involving Russia.
